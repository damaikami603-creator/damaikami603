The Human Heart

Discover fascinating scientific facts about your heart – the tireless engine of life

Key Scientific Facts

Beats ~100,000 times per day

Your heart beats about 100,000 times daily, pumping blood throughout your body non-stop. Over a lifetime, that's more than 2.5–3 billion beats.

Pumps ~2,000 gallons daily

It pumps around 2,000 gallons (7,500 liters) of blood each day, enough to fill a small swimming pool.

Size of a fist (adult)

An adult heart is roughly the size of two clasped hands or a large fist, weighing 7–15 ounces (200–425 grams).

4 Chambers & Valves

It has four chambers (two atria, two ventricles) and four main valves that ensure one-way blood flow.

Own "Little Brain"

The heart has its own nervous system with ~40,000 neurons, allowing it to sense and respond independently.

Produces Hormones

It releases hormones like atrial natriuretic peptide to help regulate blood pressure and fluid balance.
