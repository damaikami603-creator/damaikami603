Discover the Heart: Scientific Facts

Welcome to Heart Facts

The human heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the body, beating about 100,000 times a day. Explore 10 pages of verified scientific facts on its structure, function, and more.[web:1][web:3]

Quick Facts

  • Your heart beats 40 million times a year.
  • It pumps 2,000 gallons of blood daily.
  • The right side handles deoxygenated blood; left side, oxygenated.[web:3]

Heart Anatomy

Heart Anatomy

The heart has four chambers: two atria (upper) and two ventricles (lower). The interventricular septum is thicker than the interatrial one for higher pressure.[web:1]

  • Fibrous skeleton supports valves and blocks electrical conduction.
  • Right ventricle pumps to lungs; left to body.[web:1][web:3]
  • Size of a fist, weighs ~10 oz in women, 11 oz in men.
